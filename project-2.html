Defend the planning profession in Kenya.

Champion Planners Cohesion and Unity of Purpose

Our Achievements

o Facilitated the professional planner's dialogue in Pwani, South Rift, and Western Regions;
o Actively engaged with professional associations on the unity of Purpose (AAK-TP, TCPAK, and KYP);
o Championed East Africa Planners’ Network to enable cross-border practice and welfare;

Intention

o Champion for planners cohesion workshop (KIP, AAK-TP, TCPAK, and KYP); and
o Rejuvenate Planners’ professional codes and ethics (professional baptism)

Require Fulfilment of Constitutional and Statutory Provisions for Planning

Our Achievements

o Partnered with CoG, NLC, and MOLPWHUD to sensitize and induct Governors, Planning Authorities, relevant CECM, and Directors on statutory provisions for planning;
o Supported mandated institutions to establish required boards, ad-hoc committees, and liaison Committees; and
o Reached out to relevant Committees in the Kenya Parliament (Senate and National Assembly).

Intention

o Lobby for effective legislative review of conflicting and outdated legislation on planning (PPRA, PLUPA, CGA, UACA, PFMA);
o Enhance sensitization of counties and mandated institutions on constitutional and statutory provisions for planning;
o Institute an Annual Status of Planning Assessment Report to determine and encourage planning statutory compliance;
o Conduct Regional Workshops to discuss solutions for planning statutory compliance;
o Support the development of an index on all relevant legal provisions and regulations to enhance planning practice in Kenya; and
o Compel Counties and mandated institutions to fulfill constitutional and statutory provisions for planning.

Champion Planning Curriculum Review and Accreditation

Our Achievements

o Enhanced partnership to support review of planning curriculum (CAP, UN-HABITAT, WWF-Kenya, MoLPHUD);
o Conducted Workshop with UoN-DURP to support review of the Urban and Regional Planning Curriculum (Undergraduate, Master, and Ph.D.); and
o Outreach to Pwani University.

Intention

o Enhance outreach to all planning schools through KIP Regional branches;
o Sensitise mandated institutions on minimum for admission into planning schools (Planning Schools, PPRB, KUCCPS);
o Review and recommend adequate planning units, space requirements, studio requirements, tools, technical capacity, and qualification of lecturers in planning schools;
o Support inclusion of Registered and Practising planners in Planning students’ discourse;
o Support the requirement and placement of planning students in attachment to relevant planning practice organizations both public and private; and
o Champion Review, Accreditation, and Recognition of valid planning schools/courses (Planning Schools, CUE);

Foster Meaningful Allies with Sectoral Mandated Institutions and Agencies

Our Achievements

o Enhanced partnership and collaboration with CoG (Committees on Lands and Urban Development, Infrastructure, and Energy);
o Initiated partnerships with regional Economic Blocs (JKP, LREB);
o Enhanced collaboration and Partnerships with relevant mandated agencies and institutions (MoLPWHUD, NLC, NEMA, KURA, KeRRA, KIPPRA, KEFRI);
o Supported joint position and outreach with professionals in the built environment to Counties on the preparation of relevant plans (CSP, Ward Plans, ISUDPs);
o Championed the formation of the Real Estate and Construction Network (RECONET);
o Enhanced partnerships and collaboration with Private Sector (KEPSA, KPDA, ACORN, ROYAL GROUP);
o Elicited support within APSEA advocating for and nominating planning professionals in the land sector;
o Organized relevant Sector based Conferences and Forums (1st Urbanisation Conference, KUF, UN-HABITAT II); and
o An enhanced partnership that recognizes planning professionals with donor and multilateral agencies (UN-HABITAT, UNEP, FAO, World Bank, WIOMSA).

Intention

o Enhance outreach to Auditor General;
o Enhance outreach to Commission on Revenue Allocation;
o Push for adequate recognition of relevant planning disciplines;
o Foster more meaningful partnerships with stakeholders in the Built environments;
o Support an integrated and inclusive approach to planning projects; and
o Organize relevant joint conferences/Workshops on Planning for Sector-Specific solutions (1st Food Security Conference, 1st Climate Action and Renewable Energy Conference)
