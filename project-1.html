Support Graduate Planners’ Engagement and Employment

Our Achievements

o Encouraged graduates to practice through competition in the preparation of the Embarigo Primary School Master Plan;
o Engaged Graduates in the successful preparation of the Murang’a University of Technology Master Plan;
o Lobbied counties in the activated Regional branches to recognize Graduate Planners to submit development Applications on behalf of registered planners;
o Conducted over 10 inductions that encouraged the registration of experienced graduate members; and
o Facilitated the 1st Young Planners Workshop.

Intention

1. Ensure representation of graduate members in the KIP governing council;
2. Guarantee Job opportunities for Graduate planners;
3. Lobby for worthwhile pay and privileges for planning assistants;
4. Engage Graduate planners in the County Planning and Development Project;
5. Support Review of PPRA to enable PPRB recognition and prompt registration of qualified Graduate planners as Planning Associates; and
6. Require counties to solely recognize Graduate Planners to submit development Applications on behalf of registered planners.
7. Lobby for employers to pay graduate subscriptions and annual conference participation/facilitation fees;
8. Champion for a Bi-Annual Young Planners Conference;
9. Conduct Exhibitions and Showcase Planning Assignments; and
10. Champion for Awards, Certification, and Scholarships for exemplary Graduate Planners.
11. Organise Relevant, Contemporary, and Informative CPDs targeting Graduate Planners/Planning Assistants
12. Establish a KIP Graduates database to track professional progress;
13. Engagement of young planners in steering and commemoration of Important Planning events i.e. World Town Planning Day;
